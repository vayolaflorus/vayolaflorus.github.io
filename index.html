What I do

I support organizations in two connected ways. Some teams need one. Many need both.

1) Building staff capacity

Who this is for

Program managers, frontline staff, and leadership teams who are expected to use digital tools as part of their work.

What this looks like
  • Practical staff training
  • Role-specific workshops
  • Support as teams adapt to new tools
  • Space to slow down, ask questions, and build confidence

This work often includes conversations about AI. I help staff understand what AI can and can’t do, where it fits into their work, and where it doesn’t — so decisions aren’t driven by pressure or fear of falling behind.

When staff feel prepared, decisions feel safer and day-to-day work becomes more manageable.

2) Supporting public-facing programs

Who this is for

Organizations running programs that help community members build digital skills or access technology.

What this looks like
  • Program design and planning
  • Facilitating or supporting participant training
  • Adapting content for different language levels
  • Making sure programs work in real life, not just on paper

This includes being thoughtful about how technology — including AI — is introduced and discussed. I support teams in setting realistic expectations and using clear language.

Programs are stronger when teaching technology is guided by real outcomes with real audiences in mind.

About

I’m Vayola Florus. I work with nonprofit and public-sector organizations navigating increased pressure to use technology responsibly.

I’ve seen how often teams are asked to adopt new tools without enough time, guidance, or support — and how that pressure affects both staff and the people programs are meant to serve.

My approach is practical and people-centered. I focus on how technology shows up in everyday work — in meetings, programs, classrooms, and community spaces.

I believe technology should support staff, increase access, and strengthen programs — not overwhelm people or create new barriers.
